Interview Questions

Prepare for your Cognizant GenC Next interview with these commonly asked questions across different categories:

Explain object-oriented programming principles like inheritance and polymorphism.

Inheritance: Inheritance is a mechanism where a new class (derived class) inherits properties and behaviors from an existing class (base class). This promotes code reusability and establishes an is-a relationship between classes.

Polymorphism: Polymorphism allows objects of different classes to be treated as objects of a common superclass. It enables a single interface to represent different underlying forms (data types). The two types of polymorphism are:

  • Compile-time polymorphism (Method Overloading): Multiple methods with the same name but different parameters.
  • Runtime polymorphism (Method Overriding): Subclass provides a specific implementation for a method already defined in its superclass.
What is the difference between a stack and a queue?

Stack:

  • Follows Last In First Out (LIFO) principle
  • Elements are added and removed from the same end (top)
  • Operations: push (add) and pop (remove)
  • Example: Function call stack, undo operation in text editors

Queue:

  • Follows First In First Out (FIFO) principle
  • Elements are added at one end (rear) and removed from the other end (front)
  • Operations: enqueue (add) and dequeue (remove)
  • Example: Print queue, task scheduling
Explain the concept of database normalization.

Database normalization is the process of organizing data in a database to reduce redundancy and improve data integrity. It involves dividing large tables into smaller ones and defining relationships between them.

The main forms of normalization are:

  • First Normal Form (1NF): Eliminate repeating groups and ensure atomic values (values cannot be divided further).
  • Second Normal Form (2NF): Meet 1NF requirements and remove partial dependencies (non-key attributes must depend on the entire primary key).
  • Third Normal Form (3NF): Meet 2NF requirements and remove transitive dependencies (non-key attributes must not depend on other non-key attributes).
  • Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF): A stronger version of 3NF where every determinant must be a candidate key.
Tell me about yourself.

This is often the opening question. Keep your answer concise (1-2 minutes) and focus on your education, relevant skills, projects, and career aspirations. Structure your answer as follows:

  • Introduction: Brief overview of your educational background.
  • Skills & Strengths: Highlight technical skills and personal strengths relevant to the role.
  • Experience: Mention key projects or internships that demonstrate your capabilities.
  • Career Goals: Express your interest in the role and how it aligns with your career aspirations.

Avoid personal details unrelated to your professional life.

Why do you want to join Cognizant?

Research Cognizant before your interview and mention specific aspects that attract you to the company, such as:

  • Global presence and diverse work environment
  • Opportunity to work with cutting-edge technologies
  • Strong focus on innovation and digital transformation
  • Excellent learning and growth opportunities
  • Company culture and values that align with your own

Be specific about how Cognizant's GenC Next program will help you achieve your career goals and how you can contribute to the company's success.

Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

This question assesses your career planning and ambition. Your answer should be:

  • Realistic: Set achievable goals based on the typical career progression at Cognizant.
  • Ambitious: Show drive and desire to grow professionally.
  • Relevant: Align your goals with opportunities available at Cognizant.

A good response might include advancing to a senior technical role, developing expertise in specific technologies, taking on leadership responsibilities, or contributing to significant projects within the company.

Describe a challenging project you worked on and how you overcame obstacles.

Use the STAR method to structure your answer:

  • Situation: Briefly describe the project and its objectives.
  • Task: Explain your specific responsibilities and the challenges you faced.
  • Action: Detail the steps you took to address the challenges, emphasizing your problem-solving approach, technical skills, and collaboration with others.
  • Result: Share the positive outcomes of your actions, including what you learned from the experience.

Focus on demonstrating skills valued at Cognizant, such as problem-solving, adaptability, teamwork, and technical expertise.

How do you handle tight deadlines and pressure?

Describe your approach to managing stress and meeting deadlines. Include specific examples from your academic or work experience where you successfully handled pressure. Mention strategies like:

  • Prioritization and breaking tasks into smaller, manageable parts
  • Time management techniques you use effectively
  • Maintaining a positive attitude and focus under pressure
  • Seeking help or collaborating when necessary
  • Learning from past experiences to improve future performance

Emphasize your ability to remain calm and focused under pressure while delivering quality work on time.

Tell me about a time when you had to learn a new technology quickly.

This question assesses your adaptability and learning agility. Again, use the STAR method:

  • Situation: Describe the context that required you to learn something new.
  • Task: Explain why learning this technology was necessary and the timeframe you had.
  • Action: Detail your learning approach, including resources used (documentation, tutorials, online courses), practice methods, and how you sought help when needed.
  • Result: Share how you successfully applied the new knowledge, the outcome of the project, and any recognition received.

Highlight your self-motivation, resourcefulness, and systematic approach to learning new skills—qualities highly valued in the fast-paced tech environment at Cognizant.

Selection Process

A comprehensive test that evaluates your aptitude, logical reasoning, and technical knowledge.

Key Components
  • Quantitative Aptitude: Mathematical and logical reasoning questions
  • Verbal Ability: English language proficiency assessment
  • Technical MCQs: Questions on programming concepts, data structures, and algorithms
  • Coding Questions: 1-2 programming problems to solve
2

Technical Interview

An in-depth assessment of your technical knowledge, problem-solving skills, and project experience.

Focus Areas
  • Core programming concepts and data structures
  • Object-oriented programming principles
  • Database concepts and SQL queries
  • System design basics
  • Projects mentioned in your resume
3

HR Interview

Evaluates your communication skills, cultural fit, and career aspirations.

Common Topics
  • Background and educational qualifications
  • Career goals and aspirations
  • Knowledge about Cognizant and the industry
  • Willingness to relocate and work in shifts
  • Behavioral questions to assess soft skills
4

Final Selection & Offer

Based on your performance in all previous rounds, Cognizant will make the final selection decision.

Important Notes
