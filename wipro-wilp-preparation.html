About Wipro WILP

The Wipro Work Integrated Learning Program (WILP) is a standardized assessment conducted by Wipro. It serves as a comprehensive screening process that identifies capable graduates to join Wipro and its client companies.

WILP is a unique opportunity for B.Sc. and BCA graduates to earn a fully sponsored M.Tech degree while working at Wipro. This program is designed in collaboration with prestigious institutions like BITS Pilani, offering students the perfect blend of academic learning and practical work experience.

Eligibility Criteria

Before applying for Wipro WILP, ensure you meet the following eligibility criteria:

Criteria Requirements
Academic Criteria 60% or 6 CGPA throughout all academic studies, including the highest qualification, up to the latest semester for which results have been declared at the time of the test process.
Highest Qualification Exclusively for 2025 batch with BCA or B.Sc. in Computer Science, Information Technology, Mathematics, Statistics, Electronics, or Physics.
Mathematics Requirement Core Mathematics must have been studied as a subject in Graduation; Business Maths and Applied Maths will not be considered as Core Mathematics.
Age Limit No age limit
Backlog/Arrears One backlog is permitted at the time of Online Assessment, which should be cleared along with the 6th semester.
Education Gap Maximum 3 years of education gap between 10th standard and the start of Graduation. No gaps during Graduation.
Course Types Only full-time courses (Part-time/Correspondence courses not considered)
Work Experience Candidates with prior work experience of up to 2 years are eligible (Applicable only for postgraduate students)

Additional Requirements

Citizenship

Should be an Indian citizen or should carry a PIO or OCI card, if holding a passport from any other country. Bhutan and Nepal nationals need to submit their citizenship certificates.

College Attendance

Candidates must not have any pending attendance requirements with the college.

Gap Between Applications

Rest period of at least 3 months between the previous and present applications of a candidate.

Registration Process

Follow these steps to register for the Wipro WILP program:

1

Visit Official Website

Navigate to the official Wipro WILP website and click on "Register / Sign In" to access the registration page.

Important Tips
  • Use a frequently checked email ID as all communications will be sent there.
  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection during the registration process.
2

Create Superset Account

Enter your email ID to create a Superset account, which is the platform used for Wipro's recruitment process.

Remember
  • Keep your login credentials secure for future access.
  • Use a professional email address rather than a casual one.
3

Fill Application Form

Complete all sections of the application form, including personal details, educational background, and any work experience.

Form Sections
  • Basic Details: Name, date of birth, gender, contact information
  • Current Education: University, graduation program details
  • Previous Education: 10th and 12th standard results
  • Work Experience: Any relevant internships or jobs (if applicable)
4

Upload Documents

Upload a professional profile photo and all necessary documents as required by the application process.

Document Checklist
  • Professional profile photo (formal attire against a plain background)
  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • ID proof
  • Updated resume in PDF format
5

Submit Application

Review all information carefully, agree to the terms and conditions, then click "Agree and Apply" to submit your application.

After Submission
  • Save your application ID for future reference.
  • Check your email for a confirmation message.
  • Regularly monitor your email for updates on the selection process.

Interview Questions

Prepare for your Wipro WILP interview with these commonly asked questions across different categories:

Explain the difference between stack and queue data structures.

Stack:

  • Follows Last In First Out (LIFO) principle
  • Elements are added and removed from the same end (top)
  • Operations: push (add) and pop (remove)
  • Example: Function call stack, undo operation in text editors

Queue:

  • Follows First In First Out (FIFO) principle
  • Elements are added at one end (rear) and removed from the other end (front)
  • Operations: enqueue (add) and dequeue (remove)
  • Example: Print queue, task scheduling
What is object-oriented programming and explain its key principles?

Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) is a programming paradigm based on the concept of "objects" which can contain data and code. The data is in the form of fields (attributes), and the code is in the form of procedures (methods).

Key Principles:

  • Encapsulation: Bundling data and methods that operate on that data within a single unit (class), and restricting access to some of the object's components.
  • Inheritance: Mechanism where a new class inherits properties and behaviors from an existing class, promoting code reusability.
  • Polymorphism: Ability of different classes to be treated as instances of the same class through inheritance, allowing methods to do different things based on the object it is acting upon.
  • Abstraction: Hiding complex implementation details and showing only the necessary features of an object.
Explain the concept of cloud computing and its service models.

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence—over the Internet ("the cloud") to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

Service Models:

  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS): Provides virtualized computing resources over the internet. Examples: AWS EC2, Google Compute Engine, Microsoft Azure VM.
  • Platform as a Service (PaaS): Provides a platform allowing customers to develop, run, and manage applications without dealing with the complexity of building and maintaining the infrastructure. Examples: AWS Elastic Beanstalk, Google App Engine, Microsoft Azure App Services.
  • Software as a Service (SaaS): Delivers software applications over the internet, on-demand and typically on a subscription basis. Examples: Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Salesforce.
Tell me about yourself.

This is often the opening question. Keep your answer concise (1-2 minutes) and focus on your education, relevant skills, projects, and career aspirations. Structure your answer as follows:

  • Introduction: Brief overview of your educational background.
  • Skills & Strengths: Highlight technical skills and personal strengths relevant to the role.
  • Experience: Mention key projects or internships that demonstrate your capabilities.
  • Career Goals: Express your interest in the role and how it aligns with your career aspirations.

Avoid personal details unrelated to your professional life.

Why do you want to join Wipro WILP?

Research Wipro before your interview and mention specific aspects that attract you to the company and the WILP program, such as:

  • Opportunity to earn an M.Tech degree while gaining practical work experience
  • Wipro's global presence and diverse work environment
  • Company's focus on innovation and digital transformation
  • Structured learning and career growth opportunities
  • Collaboration with prestigious educational institutions like BITS Pilani

Be specific about how the WILP program will help you achieve your career goals and how you can contribute to Wipro's success.

How do you feel about the service agreement/bond?

This question assesses your commitment to the program. Your answer should demonstrate:

  • Understanding: Show that you understand the terms of the agreement (60 months duration, early exit penalty of ₹75,000 on a pro-rata basis).
  • Perspective: Frame it as a fair exchange for the valuable education and experience you'll receive.
  • Commitment: Express your intention to complete the full term and grow with the company.

Example response: "I view the service agreement as a fair commitment considering Wipro is investing in my education and professional development. I'm looking for long-term growth opportunities, so I'm fully prepared to honor this commitment and contribute to Wipro's success during this period and beyond."

Describe a challenging project you worked on and how you overcame obstacles.

Use the STAR method to structure your answer:

  • Situation: Briefly describe the project and its objectives.
  • Task: Explain your specific responsibilities and the challenges you faced.
  • Action: Detail the steps you took to address the challenges, emphasizing your problem-solving approach, technical skills, and collaboration with others.
  • Result: Share the positive outcomes of your actions, including what you learned from the experience.

Focus on demonstrating skills valued at Wipro, such as problem-solving, adaptability, teamwork, and technical expertise.

How do you handle tight deadlines and pressure?

Describe your approach to managing stress and meeting deadlines. Include specific examples from your academic or work experience where you successfully handled pressure. Mention strategies like:

  • Prioritization and breaking tasks into smaller, manageable parts
  • Time management techniques you use effectively
  • Maintaining a positive attitude and focus under pressure
  • Seeking help or collaborating when necessary
  • Learning from past experiences to improve future performance

Emphasize your ability to remain calm and focused under pressure while delivering quality work on time.

Tell me about a time when you had to learn a new technology quickly.

This question assesses your adaptability and learning agility. Again, use the STAR method:

  • Situation: Describe the context that required you to learn something new.
  • Task: Explain why learning this technology was necessary and the timeframe you had.
  • Action: Detail your learning approach, including resources used (documentation, tutorials, online courses), practice methods, and how you sought help when needed.
  • Result: Share how you successfully applied the new knowledge, the outcome of the project, and any recognition received.

Highlight your self-motivation, resourcefulness, and systematic approach to learning new skills—qualities highly valued in the fast-paced tech environment at Wipro.
